Strategic Activation

Power Loyalists

30.8% of customers
Customer Profile: Highly engaged customers with strong loyalty behavior, recurring store interaction and relevant basket value. Business Role: They support stable revenue and represent a strong base for relationship expansion, cross-selling and loyalty reinforcement. Recommended Action: Deepen relationship and upsell. Launch a VIP tier with early access, exclusive events, referral rewards and cross-sell opportunities across adjacent premium categories. Suggested KPIs: Average basket growth, cross-sell uptake, referral participation and retention rate.

Premium Buyers

12.3% of customers
Customer Profile: Smaller but high-value customer segment with premium-oriented shopping missions, strong basket value and high potential for margin contribution. Business Role: Although they represent a smaller share of customers, they generate 27% of total revenue, making them strategically important for profitability and premium-category growth. Recommended Action: Retention and churn prevention. Protect this segment through exclusive product previews, early access to seasonal lines and premium loyalty benefits. Suggested KPIs: Churn rate, retention rate, premium-category spend and average order value.

Regular Shoppers

43.1% of customers
Customer Profile: Largest customer group. Routine-oriented shoppers with strong relevance for volume growth, but requiring targeted incentives to increase frequency and strengthen engagement. Business Role: This segment represents the biggest opportunity for scalable CRM activation because even small behavioral improvements can generate meaningful business impact. Recommended Action: Increase visit frequency. Use bundle offers, personalized reminders after inactivity, loyalty upgrade incentives and category-based promotions. Suggested KPIs: Visit frequency, campaign response rate, loyalty upgrade conversion and repeat purchase rate.

Churned Customers

13.7% of customers
Customer Profile: Disengaged or at-risk customers with lower recent activity and weaker shopping continuity. This segment requires reactivation before value is permanently lost. Business Role: They represent a defensive CRM priority: the goal is to recover inactive customers and understand the reasons behind their drop-off. Recommended Action: Win-back reactivation. Implement a 3-touch reactivation journey combining reminder emails, time-limited discount codes and churn surveys to understand exit reasons. Suggested KPIs: Reactivation rate, voucher redemption, survey response rate and recovered revenue.
